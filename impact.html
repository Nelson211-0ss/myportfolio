Impact Areas

From Access to Action

The impact strategy combines product access, community training, and public advocacy to reduce period poverty and keep girls in school.

Last-Mile Distribution

Mobile kiosks and local networks bring affordable menstrual hygiene products closer to girls and women in hard-to-reach communities.

Skills and Production

Community workshops teach reusable pad and liquid soap making. Public reports from Soroti noted large practical trainings that included women, girls, and men.

Education Retention

Reducing period-related absenteeism helps girls remain active in class and continue their academic progression.

Stigma Reduction

Advocacy campaigns encourage open conversations on menstruation and strengthen supportive attitudes among men and community leaders.

Program Architecture

01 - Access

Affordable reusable products are brought closer to communities through local distribution and practical demonstrations.

02 - Capacity

Master training and peer learning help communities produce pads and hygiene soap while spreading knowledge sustainably.

03 - Advocacy

Rights-based campaigns and public commentary promote lasting social norm change around menstruation and sanitation.
