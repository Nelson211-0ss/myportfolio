About Eric

Leadership Profile

Eric Omondi is the founder of Esonga Menstrual Care in Busia, Uganda. His work centers on practical, affordable, and sustainable menstrual hygiene solutions for women and girls in rural communities where period poverty remains a major barrier to education and dignity.

Enterprise Focus

Esonga Menstrual Care emphasizes last-mile distribution, including mobile kiosk access to eco-friendly and reusable products.

Advocacy Voice

Eric challenges menstrual stigma through open public education and champions inclusion as a "menstruating man" ally.

MBA Qualification

Holds an MBA qualification that strengthens leadership, strategy, and social enterprise execution for community impact.

Public Thought Leadership

Eric has published commentary calling for better sanitation systems, framing sanitation as a human right and a development priority.

Human Rights Lens

Coverage highlights his framing of period poverty as a dignity, education, and health rights issue for girls and women.

Expanded Professional Narrative

Eric's leadership approach is rooted in community proximity: listening to what girls, parents, and local leaders identify as the biggest menstrual health barriers, then designing practical solutions they can replicate locally.

Through Esonga Menstrual Care and related field programs, his work links product access with confidence building. Training sessions are not only technical; they also build safe spaces for discussing menstruation, dignity, and social stigma.

His public engagement reflects a wider development lens, connecting menstrual health to education, sanitation systems, local economies, and long-term gender equity outcomes.
