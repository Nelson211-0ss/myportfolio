About Eric

Leadership profile

Eric Omondi is the founder of Esonga Menstrual Care, rooted in Busia, Uganda. He builds hybrid programmes that braid products, fearless public menstruation discourse, and plans district teams can graft onto adolescent health mandates.

Narratives Nexus Media and The Cooperator carried — plus op-eds Eric authors in outlets like the Daily Monitor — document how he frames reusables, soap skills, and rights language as one braided response to period poverty.

Organisation

Enterprise focus

Mobile kiosks, neighbour-stocked replenishment rhythms, inventory visibility for donors — all designed so menstrual shopping mirrors how families already acquire staple goods.

Voice

Advocacy & male allyship

Eric uses culturally fluent facilitation — humour where it earns trust — to draw men into menstrual budgeting at barazas while protecting girls from becoming media props.

Credentials

Qualifications & public voice

Op-edsSanitation and neglect critiques that tie menstrual deprivation to statewide WASH accountability.

Non-negotiables

Core principles

  • Dignity — design for bodily autonomy pathways, not gimmicks.
  • Ownership — neighbours lead; outsiders stage inputs briefly.
  • Affordability — reusables and soap tied to refill economics communities sustain.
  • Retention signals — aggregate attendance ethically; celebrate SMC wins anonymously.
